Composition

Acrylic is a synthetic polymer made from polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA). It is created through a process called polymerization, where small molecules called monomers join together to form long chains or polymers.

Transparency

Acrylic has excellent optical clarity, similar to glass. It allows over 90% of light to pass through, making it a popular choice for applications requiring transparency, such as windows, display cases, and lenses.

Weather Resistance

Acrylic is highly resistant to UV radiation and weathering, making it suitable for outdoor applications. It does not yellow or degrade when exposed to sunlight, unlike some other plastics.

Acrylic Types

There are different types of acrylic with varying properties. Cast acrylic is produced by pouring liquid acrylic into molds, resulting in sheets with uniform thickness and excellent surface finish. Extruded acrylic is formed by pushing melted acrylic through a die, producing sheets with consistent thickness but slightly lower optical clarity than cast acrylic.

Acrylic vs. Glass

Acrylic is often chosen as an alternative to glass due to its lightweight nature, impact resistance, and lower cost. Compared to glass, acrylic is less likely to shatter and can be up to 17 times more impact-resistant. However, acrylic is more prone to scratching than glass, so care must be taken to protect its surface.

Recycling

Acrylic is recyclable. The recycling process involves breaking down the acrylic into its raw monomer form, which can be used to produce new acrylic products.

Strength and Durability

Despite being lightweight, acrylic is relatively strong and impact-resistant. It is more durable than glass, making it less prone to shattering. Acrylic sheets are commonly used in construction, automotive parts, and protective barriers.

Lightweight

Acrylic is lighter than glass, which makes it easier to handle, transport, and install.

Wide Range of Colors

Acrylic is available in a vast array of colors, including transparent, translucent, and opaque options.

Chemical Resistance

Acrylic exhibits good resistance to many chemicals, including acids and bases.
